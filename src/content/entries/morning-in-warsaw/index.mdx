Today I walked through the old town before the cafés opened. The cobblestones
were still wet from the night, and the whole square belonged to the pigeons and
to me.

There is a particular quality to morning light in Warsaw — low, golden, and
unhurried. It slips between the rebuilt façades of the Rynek Starego Miasta and
catches the edges of everything.

> The city that was rebuilt from photographs now writes its own light again.

I stayed until the first tram rattled past and the spell broke.
